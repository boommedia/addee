# Social Media Autopilot: Complete Guide

**Bloggy's one-click publishing tool for Google Business, LinkedIn, Medium, and Dev.to**

## What It Is

Social Media Autopilot is a $15/mo add-on that lets you publish blog posts directly to multiple social platforms without leaving the Bloggy editor. Generate once, publish to multiple platforms with a single click.

Instead of:
1. Finishing a blog post
2. Manually logging into Google Business to share an update
3. Logging into LinkedIn and copying the text
4. Switching to Medium and reformatting everything
5. Posting to Dev.to separately

**With Social Media Autopilot:**
1. Generate a blog post in Bloggy
2. Click "Publish" on each platform you want
3. Done — your content is live everywhere

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Enable the Add-on

- Go to **Billing** in your Bloggy dashboard
- Scroll to **Social Media Autopilot** (featured section at the top)
- Click **"Add $15/mo"**
- You'll see a new "Social Media Autopilot" section appear in all your posts

### Step 2: Connect Your Platforms

Each platform requires a one-time connection. Choose which platforms make sense for your audience:

## Platform: Google Business Profile

**Best for:** Local businesses, service providers, stores, restaurants
**Reaches:** Google Search, Google Maps, local pack
**Posting tip:** Keep text short (100-200 chars), include a clear image

### Connecting Google Business

1. Open any blog post
2. Scroll to **Social Media Autopilot** section
3. Click **"Connect Google Business"**
4. A popup opens — sign in with the Google account that manages your business
5. Authorize Bloggy to post on your behalf
6. Once connected, you'll see your business name and a "Publish Now" button

### Publishing to Google Business

1. Open or generate a blog post
2. In the Social Media Autopilot section:
   - **Optionally add an image** (landscape or square, 400x400px minimum)
   - Click **"Publish Now"**
3. Your post appears as a Google Business update
4. Includes a link back to your WordPress blog post

**What appears:** Title, brief description, featured image, and a "Learn more" link to your full blog post

**Timing:** Posts go live immediately

## Platform: LinkedIn

**Best for:** B2B companies, service businesses, agencies, professionals
**Reaches:** LinkedIn feed, your followers, search
**Posting tip:** Add a hook or summary text before the link — LinkedIn's algorithm rewards content-rich posts

### Connecting LinkedIn

1. Open any blog post
2. Scroll to **Social Media Autopilot** section
3. Click **"Connect LinkedIn"**
4. A popup opens — sign in with your LinkedIn account
5. Authorize Bloggy to post to your company page
6. Once connected, you'll see "Connected" status

### Publishing to LinkedIn

1. In the Social Media Autopilot section:
   - **Optionally add an image** (1200x628px works best)
   - Click **"Publish to LinkedIn"**
2. Your post goes live on your company page
3. Includes a rich preview of your blog post

**What appears:** LinkedIn post with image thumbnail, article title, description, and link to full post

**Timing:** Posts go live immediately

**Pro tip:** LinkedIn rewards posts with comments. Consider adding a question in the post text to drive engagement.

## Platform: Medium

**Best for:** Technical content, long-form articles, thought leadership
**Reaches:** Medium's audience, email subscribers, search
**Setup requirement:** Medium Integration Token (one-time setup in Settings)

### Setup: Add Your Medium Token

1. Go to **Settings** → **Integrations** tab
2. Find **Medium**
3. Visit `medium.com/me/settings/security` (while logged in)
4. Scroll to "Integration tokens"
5. Click "Create new token"
6. Copy the token
7. Paste into Bloggy's Medium field
8. **Save**

### Publishing to Medium

1. Open a blog post
2. Scroll to **Social Media Autopilot**
3. Click **"Publish to Medium"**
4. The full blog post publishes to Medium
5. A canonical URL is automatically set to your WordPress post (this tells search engines the original is on your site)

**What appears:** Full article with title, featured image, content, and canonical URL

**Timing:** Posts appear on Medium instantly

**Why Medium?** Medium has millions of readers actively looking for great content. Your posts can get discovered by new audiences beyond your existing blog readers.

## Platform: Dev.to

**Best for:** Technical blogs, programming tutorials, developer audience
**Reaches:** Dev.to's 2M+ developers, email subscribers, search
**Setup requirement:** Dev.to API Key (one-time setup in Settings)

### Setup: Add Your Dev.to API Key

1. Go to **Settings** → **Integrations** tab
2. Find **Dev.to**
3. Visit `dev.to/settings/account` (while logged in)
4. Scroll to "DEV Community API Keys"
5. Click "Generate new API key"
6. Copy the key
7. Paste into Bloggy's Dev.to field
8. **Save**

### Publishing to Dev.to

1. Open a blog post
2. Scroll to **Social Media Autopilot**
3. Click **"Publish to Dev.to"**
4. The full blog post publishes to Dev.to
5. A canonical URL is automatically set to your WordPress post

**What appears:** Full article with title, featured image, content, tags, and canonical URL

**Timing:** Posts appear on Dev.to instantly

**Pro tip:** Tags are critical on Dev.to. Use relevant tags like #javascript, #tutorial, #webdev to help developers find your content.

## Publishing Strategy

### What to Publish Where

| Platform | Content Type | Frequency | Goal |
|----------|-------------|-----------|------|
| **Google Business** | Local news, updates, promotions | 2-3/week | Drive foot traffic & local visibility |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B insights, case studies, thought leadership | 1-2/week | Build authority, generate leads |
| **Medium** | In-depth articles, tutorials, stories | 1-2/week | Reach new audiences |
| **Dev.to** | Technical tutorials, code snippets, dev insights | 1-2/week | Reach developer community |

### Example Publishing Workflow

**Monday:** Publish a "10 Tips for X" blog post
- Google Business: Quick tip format with image
- LinkedIn: B2B angle with case study link
- Medium: Full article to reach thought leaders
- Dev.to: Skip (not technical)

**Wednesday:** Publish a technical tutorial
- LinkedIn: Skip (not relevant)
- Dev.to: Full technical guide with code
- Medium: Developer audience also appreciates deep dives
- Google Business: Skip (not local-relevant)

## Troubleshooting

### "Connection Failed" When Clicking Connect

1. **Check popup blocker:** Your browser may be blocking the OAuth popup
   - Look for a popup blocked notification in your address bar
   - Allow popups for bloggy.online

2. **Try incognito mode:** Sometimes cached cookies cause issues
   - Open an incognito/private window
   - Try connecting again

3. **Clear browser cache:** Go to browser settings → Clear browsing data
   - Select "Cached images and files"
   - Clear and try again

4. **Wrong account?** Make sure you're signing in with the correct Google/LinkedIn account that manages the business

### Posts Won't Show on Platform

1. **Wait 60 seconds:** Sometimes there's a delay before posts appear
2. **Check the platform directly:** Log into Google Business, LinkedIn, Medium, or Dev.to to verify
3. **Permissions issue?** Ensure the account you connected has posting permissions
4. **Try again:** Click "Publish Now" again — network hiccups happen

### Image Didn't Upload

- Minimum size: 400x400 pixels
- Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP
- File size: Under 5MB
- Try a different image or re-upload

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a WordPress site to use Social Media Autopilot?**
A: For Google Business and LinkedIn, no — you only need those accounts. For Medium and Dev.to, you still need to set up integration tokens, but they work independently of WordPress.

**Q: Can I publish to some platforms but not others?**
A: Yes! Only connect the platforms you use. You'll see publish buttons only for connected platforms.

**Q: What if I want to edit a post after publishing?**
A: Each platform is independent. If you edit on your blog, it won't automatically update the social posts. You'd need to manually edit on each platform (that's why it's great to keep social posts brief and link back to the full post on your blog).

**Q: Can my team members publish too?**
A: Team members will see the Social Media Autopilot sections, but they'll use the account connections you set up. Only the account owner needs to authenticate once.

**Q: Does the $15/mo add to my plan cost?**
A: Yes. Your plan + $15/mo for Social Media Autopilot = your total monthly cost.

**Q: What happens if I cancel?**
A: The add-on stops working. Previously published posts stay on the platforms — we don't delete anything. If you re-enable, the connections remain.
