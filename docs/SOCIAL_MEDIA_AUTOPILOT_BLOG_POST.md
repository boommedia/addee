## How It Works

### Setup (5 minutes total)

1. **Enable the add-on:** Go to Billing → Add Social Media Autopilot ($15/mo)
2. **Connect your platforms:** Each platform requires a one-time 2-minute authorization
   - Google Business & LinkedIn: OAuth popup authorization
   - Medium & Dev.to: Copy your API token into Settings → Integrations

### Publishing (30 seconds)

1. Open or generate a blog post
2. Scroll to the Social Media Autopilot section
3. Add an optional image (each platform has different sizing)
4. Click "Publish to [Platform]"
5. Your post is live

That's it. No reformatting, no logging into five different platforms.

## Platform-Specific Benefits

### Google Business Profile

**Who should use it:** Local service businesses, brick-and-mortar stores, local agencies

Google Business posts appear in:
- Google Search (right sidebar of SERPs)
- Google Maps (on your business listing)
- Local pack results

**Best practices:**
- Keep text to 100-200 characters (short and punchy)
- Always include an image (80% more engagement than text-only)
- 2-3 posts per week for maximum visibility
- Use for local promotions, events, and newsworthy updates

**Example:** A plumber posts "Emergency drain service available 24/7. Call 555-0123. Free estimate." with a photo of the truck. Google shows this to anyone searching "emergency plumber near me."

---

### LinkedIn

**Who should use it:** B2B companies, agencies, professional service providers, thought leaders

LinkedIn is where business decisions happen. Your posts reach:
- Your followers and their networks
- LinkedIn's recommendation algorithm (posts with engagement get boosted)
- People searching for your industry

**Best practices:**
- Open with a hook or question (LinkedIn rewards content-rich posts)
- 100-200 character summary + link = optimal format
- Include images (1200x628px ideal) for 2x more engagement
- 1-2 posts per week on industry topics
- Engage with comments within first hour (signals quality to the algorithm)

**Example:** A marketing agency posts "3 reasons local plumbers are outranking national chains in Google Local — and you can too. Read the full analysis:" with a link to their blog post. LinkedIn users click through at 3x the rate of link-only posts.

---

### Medium

**Who should use it:** Anyone with in-depth articles, tutorials, opinion pieces, stories

Medium is built for long-form content and has:
- 200M+ monthly readers actively looking for quality articles
- Email subscribers who get notified of new posts
- Editor recommendations (top articles get featured)
- Publications you can submit to for more reach

**Best practices:**
- Post your full article (Medium users expect depth)
- Include a featured image and inline images for readability
- Use the "Series" feature for multi-part articles (algorithm boost)
- Write compelling headlines (clickthrough drives recommendations)
- 1-2 articles per week

**Why Medium matters:** A single Medium post can reach 100,000+ readers. Even if 1% click through to your blog, that's 1,000 new visitors from one article.

---

### Dev.to

**Who should use it:** Technical writers, programmers, engineering teams, coding educators

Dev.to is THE community for developers and has:
- 2M+ active developers
- Highly engaged readership (people are there specifically for technical content)
- Communities within Dev.to (Ruby, JavaScript, Python, DevOps, etc.)
- Notifications for followers

**Best practices:**
- Post your full technical article with working code examples
- Use 4-5 relevant tags (#javascript #tutorial #react #webdev)
- Write clear explanations (developers appreciate good documentation)
- Series are powerful (mark part 1, 2, 3 for algorithm boost)
- Share with relevant Dev.to communities

**Why Dev.to matters:** A single Dev.to post can get 10,000+ views and drives highly qualified traffic (developers who actually need your solution).

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to connect all 4 platforms?**
A: No. You can connect just the ones relevant to your business. You'll only see publish buttons for connected platforms.

**Q: Does publishing to social platforms count against my Bloggy plan's post limit?**
A: No. Your plan's post limit is for blog posts on your WordPress site. Social Media Autopilot publishes don't count against that limit.

**Q: Can I edit a post after publishing to social?**
A: Each platform is independent. If you update the original blog post, it won't automatically update the social posts. However, most platforms let you edit posts directly (Medium, Dev.to have edit buttons). We recommend keeping social posts as teasers that link to the full blog post — that way, the canonical source of truth is always your blog.

**Q: What if I want different content on each platform?**
A: Social Media Autopilot is designed for the same content on all platforms. If you need platform-specific variations, you can use the Repurpose tool or post manually to individual platforms.

**Q: Can my team members post too?**
A: Yes! Any team member can see the publish buttons. The connections are account-level (whoever connects them once sets it up for everyone).

**Q: What happens if I cancel the add-on?**
A: The add-on stops working, but all your previously published posts stay live on the platforms. If you re-enable it later, your connections are still there.
